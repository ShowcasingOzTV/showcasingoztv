Quorn (SA)

Quorn is a historic railway town located in South Australia‚Äôs Flinders Ranges, known for its important role in the development of rail transport and inland settlement. Established in the late 19th century, the town became a key junction for rail lines connecting Adelaide to the Far North and the Outback.

Today, Quorn is best recognised as the home of the Pichi Richi Railway, a preserved heritage railway that attracts visitors from across Australia. The town provides accommodation, services, and facilities for travellers exploring the Flinders Ranges and surrounding regional landscapes.

Surrounded by rugged ranges, scenic gorges, and open plains, Quorn offers access to some of South Australia‚Äôs most striking natural environments. Its setting within the Flinders Ranges makes it a popular base for outdoor activities, sightseeing, and heritage tourism.
