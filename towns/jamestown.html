Jamestown (SA)

Jamestown is a historic agricultural town in South Australia, located in the Northern Flinders Ranges and serving as an important regional service centre for surrounding farming communities. Positioned between the Clare Valley and the Flinders Ranges, Jamestown has long supported broadacre cropping, grazing, and rural life across the district.

Established in the late 19th century, Jamestown developed alongside the expansion of farming and the arrival of the railway, which played a key role in transporting grain, livestock, and supplies. These links helped transform Jamestown into a regional hub, supporting both local producers and travelling merchants during the height of rural expansion.
