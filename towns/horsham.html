Horsham (VIC)

Horsham is a major regional centre in western Victoria’s Wimmera region, located along the banks of the Wimmera River. Known for its strong agricultural base and role as a key service hub, Horsham supports a wide network of farming communities across north-west Victoria.

Established in the mid-19th century, Horsham developed as a centre for wheat and grain production, with fertile soils and broadacre farming shaping the surrounding landscape. Agriculture remains central to the local economy, supported by transport, processing, and regional services.

The Wimmera River runs through the heart of the city, providing a scenic focal point for recreation and community life. Walking paths, parks, and green spaces along the river create a relaxed and accessible outdoor environment.

Horsham also serves as a gateway to nearby natural attractions, including the Grampians National Park and Mount Arapiles, drawing visitors for hiking, climbing, and nature-based experiences.

The city centre offers a wide range of retail, dining, accommodation, and cultural facilities, reflecting its importance as a regional hub. Public art, gardens, and community spaces contribute to a vibrant and welcoming atmosphere.

Surrounded by farmland, waterways, and nearby ranges, Horsham represents the balance between agricultural productivity and regional lifestyle that defines the Wimmera.
