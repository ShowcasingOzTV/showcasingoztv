Tilmouth Well NT

Tilmouth Well is a historic outback roadhouse and homestead located in the Northern Territory, serving as a vital stop for travellers journeying along the Tanami Track between Alice Springs and Western Australia. With its origins as a pastoral homestead and watering point, Tilmouth Well has long played an important role in supporting movement through some of Australia‚Äôs most remote inland landscapes.

Today, Tilmouth Well is best known as a key rest and refuelling point for 4WD travellers, freight operators, and adventurers heading into the vast deserts of the Tanami. The site provides essential services, accommodation, and a sense of hospitality that is crucial for safe travel through this isolated region.

Surrounded by wide open plains and rugged outback scenery, Tilmouth Well offers visitors an authentic experience of remote Northern Territory travel. Its location marks the transition from more populated regions into the true isolation of the Tanami Desert, making it a memorable waypoint for those exploring Australia‚Äôs interior.

The Tanami Track

The Tanami Track is a remote outback route linking Alice Springs in the Northern Territory to Halls Creek in Western Australia, crossing vast desert landscapes and some of Australia‚Äôs most isolated terrain. Known for its historical significance and importance as a transport and travel corridor, the track is a key route for 4WD travellers, freight operators, and mining operations moving through the central desert region.
