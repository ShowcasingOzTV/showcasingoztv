Stawell (VIC)

Stawell is a historic regional town in western Victoria, located at the northern gateway to the Grampians National Park. Known for its gold mining heritage and strong community identity, Stawell plays an important role within the Wimmera region.

Established during the Victorian gold rush of the mid-19th century, Stawell developed as a major gold mining centre, with rich deposits driving rapid growth and shaping the town’s early economy. Mining remains a defining part of its history, with both historic sites and ongoing operations reflecting its long-standing connection to the industry.

The town is widely recognised for hosting the Stawell Gift, one of Australia’s most prestigious professional foot races. Held annually, the event attracts athletes and spectators from across the country and has become a key part of Stawell’s identity and cultural calendar.

Stawell’s location provides easy access to the Grampians, with nearby mountains, bushland, and walking trails offering opportunities for outdoor recreation. This proximity allows the town to serve as both a service centre and a base for exploring the surrounding natural attractions.

Today, Stawell offers a range of services, retail, accommodation, and community facilities, with a welcoming atmosphere and strong regional character. Heritage buildings, parks, and public spaces contribute to its appeal for both residents and visitors.

Surrounded by farmland, bushland, and mountain ranges, Stawell reflects the transition between Victoria’s agricultural plains and the rugged Grampians landscape. The town blends history, sport, and natural access in a distinctly regional setting.
