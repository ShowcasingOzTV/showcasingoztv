Euston (NSW)

Euston is a small regional town in south-western New South Wales, located on the banks of the Murray River and directly opposite the Victorian town of Robinvale. Positioned along the NSW–Victoria border, Euston forms part of a connected cross-border community shaped by river life and agriculture.

Established in the 19th century, Euston developed as a river crossing and service point for pastoral and farming districts in the surrounding region. The Murray River played a central role in transport, trade, and settlement, supporting early industries and enabling the growth of the local economy.

Today, irrigation and agriculture remain key drivers of the area, with vineyards, orchards, and cropping operations contributing to the productive Murray River corridor. The town reflects the broader Riverina and Sunraysia influence, where water access has transformed the landscape into a fertile growing region.

The Murray River continues to define Euston’s lifestyle, offering opportunities for fishing, boating, and riverside recreation. Parks and access points along the river provide a relaxed environment for both residents and visitors.

Euston’s close relationship with Robinvale creates a shared regional hub, with services, businesses, and community life extending across both sides of the river. This connection strengthens the town’s role within the wider Murray region.

Surrounded by irrigated farmland, river red gums, and open skies, Euston captures the character of Australia’s inland river communities. The town combines agricultural productivity with a laid-back riverside atmosphere.
