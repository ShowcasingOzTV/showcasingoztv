Hopetoun (VIC)

Hopetoun is a small regional town in north-west Victoria’s Mallee region, known for its agricultural heritage, relaxed lifestyle, and proximity to some of the area’s most distinctive natural attractions. Located near Lake Lascelles and within reach of Lake Tyrrell, Hopetoun offers a classic Mallee experience shaped by farming and wide-open landscapes.

Established in the late 19th century, Hopetoun developed as a service centre for surrounding wheat and grain farming districts. The fertile yet challenging Mallee environment has long supported broadacre cropping, with agriculture remaining central to the town’s identity and economy.

Lake Lascelles is a defining feature of Hopetoun, providing a scenic setting for recreation, walking, and community activities. The lake’s calm waters and open surroundings create a peaceful contrast to the dry farmland that characterises the region.

Hopetoun has also embraced regional tourism through public art and nearby natural attractions, with silo art and the nearby Lake Tyrrell drawing visitors to the area. These features highlight both the creativity and natural beauty found within the Mallee.

The town centre offers essential services, accommodation, and local businesses, supporting residents and travellers exploring north-west Victoria. Its quiet streets and welcoming atmosphere reflect the slower pace of rural life.

Surrounded by expansive plains, grain silos, and big skies, Hopetoun captures the essence of the Mallee — a region defined by resilience, agriculture, and natural beauty.
