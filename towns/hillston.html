Hillston (NSW)

Hillston is a regional town in the Riverina district of western New South Wales, situated along the banks of the Lachlan River. Known for its agricultural strength and relaxed country atmosphere, Hillston serves as an important centre for surrounding farming communities.

Established in the 19th century, Hillston developed as a river crossing and service point for pastoral activity in the region. Over time, the town evolved alongside the expansion of agriculture, with irrigation playing a key role in shaping its economy and landscape.

Today, Hillston is recognised for its diverse agricultural production, including cotton, grain, citrus, and other irrigated crops. The surrounding farmland highlights the importance of water management and the Lachlan River system in supporting regional growth.

The Lachlan River remains central to the town’s identity, providing opportunities for recreation, fishing, and riverside relaxation. Its presence also contributes to the greenery and contrast within the otherwise expansive plains of the Riverina.

Hillston offers essential services, accommodation, and local businesses, with a friendly and welcoming community. Its town centre reflects its role as a regional hub, supporting both residents and travellers moving through western New South Wales.

Surrounded by wide-open farmland and big skies, Hillston captures the character of inland Australia — where agriculture, water, and community are closely connected.
